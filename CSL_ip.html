IP & Ownership

Who Owns What — Spelled Out

Music has two copyrights: the composition (the underlying song) and the master (the recording). Here's exactly how those work in every CSL engagement.

The Two Copyrights

Every track has two distinct copyrights. Our licenses make clear which rights move to you and which stay with CSL.

  • Composition (publishing): the melody, harmony, structure, and lyrics — the "song" itself.
  • Master (sound recording): the specific recording you hear — the performance, mix, and master.

A traditional sync license grants the right to use both, but ownership stays with the publisher and label. A work-for-hire transfers ownership of both to you. We use whichever model fits the project.

Copyright Office Registration

Premium and Work-for-Hire deliverables are registered with the U.S. Copyright Office. We comply with the Office's Generative AI Registration Guidance ("GRUW process"), disclosing AI-assisted material and identifying the human-authored elements (lyrics, performance, arrangement, mix, master, editorial selection).

Catalog and Custom tiers can be upgraded with registration as an add-on at $75 per work.
